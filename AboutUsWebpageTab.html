About Our Founders

About Mariana:

I’m moving on to my senior year at Lockport Township High School where I’m involved in the

Spanish Honor Society, National Honor Society, Interact Club, and Math Team. In my free time, I

enjoy spending time with my family and friends, reading, and playing Badminton. I try to be

involved in my community as much as I can through volunteering. During the school year, I tutor

at my church every Friday and also partake in volunteering events my school’s Interact Club

hosts. Furthermore, I am very grateful for the Girls Who Code Summer Immersion Program

because thanks to the program, I am certain that I want to study Computer Science in college.

Finally, in the fall of 2017, I plan on attending Lewis University and obtaining a major in

Computer Engineering and a Double Minor in Ethnic and Cultural Studies and Spanish.





About Destiny:

I’m currently going into my junior year at Lindblom Math and Science Academy. I’m involved in

many different clubs in and outside of school; I’m currently the co-captain for our Girl’s Rugby

Team, I participate in our Learn to Read club, I’ve volunteered in many different soup kitchen

through the years also. Participating in Girl’s Who Code has given me the opportunity to expand

my knowledge in computer science and experience something new.





About Karla:

I was born in Guadalajara, Jalisco before I immigrated to the U.S at a very young age and lived

most of my life in the Northwest Suburbs of Chicago. I am the second of four children in my family.

I am currently a Senior at Palatine High School taking a packed schedule of AP classes. I first

heard about the Girls Who Code program through a girl I met at one of my other summer

programs through an organization called National Hispanic Institute(NHI). At the time, she was

starting her Girls Who Code program a few days after our NHI program ended. The more she told

me about the program the more interested I was and I made sure I applied for the next summer

program. Before I came into this program I didn’t know a thing about coding and this program has

taught me all I know. I went from having zero experience in the field to being able to code a robot

like Ari. In college, I plan to go into the STEM field and major in Computer Engineering and minor in computer programming.
