About Ari

Ari is a robot designed to educate your child with the basics such as ABC's and their 123's.

This educational robot is different from other robots on the market because Ari makes sure that your child is following along.

Ari's targeted audience is for toddlers but can also benefit children with learning disabilities such as ADHD.

This robot only continues with the activity if your child is responsing and if Ari does not hear a response for an amount of time,

she will play a tune to get the child's attention and ask if the child wants to continue the activity or move on to another one. Ari is a

robot made for child use. Ari dances with children, tells stories, and partakes in a some educational activities such as counting, and learning

the alphabet. However, Ari is not that same as many toys on the market, Ari uses a voice recognition software to taking in the child's response,

insuring that the child doesn’t get distracted and wander off towards other things, such as the t.v.

There is a barrier between tv and children which can t.v for educational purposes kinda hard. Many educational shows have a built in aspect of education,

however along the way and throughout the course of the show, your child could be learning many other skills that aren’t the best. In many educational shows things are

taught that shouldn’t actually be picked up, such as sibling disputes or recovery. It’s also very difficult for young children to actually understand the moral of the whole

story when they have to piece bits from the entire thirty minute show together.
